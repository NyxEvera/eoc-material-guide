# eoc-material-guide
Material and engineering guide for Elite Dangerous commanders.

This guide is meant to help new, returning, and veteran Commanders quickly find and collect the materials they need for engineering, unlocks, and other activities without unnecessary grinding or endless searching.

To make identification easier, materials in this guide are divided into the following categories, mostly based on the in-game classification system:

- Raw
- Manufactured
- Encoded
- Special - This category includes materials used for engineering, technology unlocks, and other special requirements within the game.

Note: Some materials require special equipment for safe transportation. Recommendations for useful modules and ship fittings can be found at the end of this guide.

<summary><strong>Raw Materials</strong></summary>

### Raw Materials

Raw Materials are divided into seven categories and four effective grades, as shown in the table below.

They can be obtained through various activities within the game. Common sources include:

- Surface prospecting with an SRV
- Mission rewards
- Mining asteroids
- Geological and biological sites on planetary surfaces

Unlike commodities, materials cannot be bought with credits.

Instead, **Material Traders** allow Commanders to exchange materials for other materials.

**Note:**

It is highly recommended to trade materials **within the same category**, preferably from higher grades down to lower grades.

Cross-trading between categories or trading upwards from low-grade to high-grade materials is extremely inefficient and should only be considered if you urgently need a specific material.

  ### Manufactured Materials
  
Manufactured Materials are divided into seven categories and five grades. Because of the high variety of materials, we will focus on Grade 5 materials only. Once you have obtained them, you can trade them down and eventually fill up on every Manufactured Material in the game.

Note: As with Raw Materials, I generally do not recommend cross-trading or up-trading, except for Exquisite Focus Crystals and Biotech Conductors.

Because there are no HGEs for these materials, you will usually need to cross-trade them from other Grade 5 Manufactured Materials.

I recommend using Imperial Shielding and/or Proto Radiolic Alloys for this purpose, as they are among the easiest Grade 5 materials to farm efficiently.

In this guide, we will focus on High Grade Emissions (HGEs), because they are one of the most reliable and repeatable ways to obtain Grade 5 Manufactured Materials.

Almost every Grade 5 Manufactured Material can be found in HGEs, with the exception of Exquisite Focus Crystals and Biotech Conductors. These are not available from HGEs and are instead primarily obtained through Mission Rewards, Encoded Emissions, or by trading materials at a Material Trader.

To find a specific Material or HGE, you need to search for star systems in a specific system state, as shown in the attached spreadsheet.

I recommend using [Inara](https://inara.cz?utm_source=chatgpt.com) for fast and convenient system searches.
Alternatively try this tool: [ED Galaxy](https://edgalaxy.net/hge?material=Military+Grade+Alloys+%26+Supercapacitors) *"works best after Thursdays"*

After arriving in the system, fly to the Nav Beacon and scan it. Once the scan is complete, return to Supercruise and check your left-hand panel for High Grade Emissions (HGEs).

Note: HGEs have a limited lifetime, which can be monitored in the top-right corner of your HUD while the signal source is targeted.

Inside the signal source you will find a debris field containing Grade 5 Manufactured Materials. Collect them, preferably with Collector Limpets.

<summary><strong>Encoded Materials</strong></summary>

### Encoded Materials
Encoded Materials are divided into seven categories and five grades. Like Raw and Manufactured Materials, they can be obtained through a variety of in-game activities.

Common sources include:

- Scanning ships
- Wake Scanning
- Data Beacons
- Mission Rewards
- Settlement Data Points
- Encoded Emissions
- Ship scans in Supercruise
- Megaships and installations

These activities usually provide random Encoded Materials, which can make farming specific materials somewhat tedious.

I recommend using Jameson's Cobra Crash Site for farming Encoded Materials.

After arriving at the site, you will find a total of nine data beacons that can be scanned. Each scan provides a variety of Encoded Materials.

Note: Scanning can be done in either your ship or your SRV.

**Reminder:** Commander John Jameson was a veteran of the First Thargoid War. He single-handedly ended the war, even though he was tricked into doing so. As a reward, he was murdered. So please, show at least a minimum of respect while robbing his grave!

Continue scanning until you have maxed out _Adaptive Encryptors Capture_ and _Atypical Encryption Archives_. If you are not maxed out after all nine beacons, relog and repeat the process. Usually, no more than two runs are required to fully max out both materials.

Once finished, head to Ray Gateway and use the Encoded Material Trader to cross-trade and down-trade into the materials you actually need.

Yes, the process is tedious. However, once you are done, you will likely have enough Encoded Materials for months, possibly even an entire year.

<summary>Thargoid-Related Materials</summary>

### Thargoid-Related Materials

This category includes items such as _Caustic Crystals_, _Thargoid Hearts_, _Caustic Shards_, and various _Tissue Samples_.

These can be obtained from Titan wrecks as well as from Non-Human Signal Sources in Thargoid space. As mentioned before, collecting them at a Titan wreck is usually much safer, while obtaining them in active Thargoid territory often requires combat.

To transport most of these safely, you will need a **Corrosion Resistant Cargo Rack**.

**Warning**

It is possible to carry them without one, but your ship will suffer continuous caustic damage that may eventually lead to its destruction.

However, be aware that travelling through Thargoid-controlled space may eventually result in a hyperdiction. Carrying Thargoid- or Guardian-related cargo can cause Thargoids to become hostile and attack your ship.

If your ship or pilot is not properly prepared for such an encounter, this will very likely result in the destruction of your ship. After the hyperdiction, Thargoids usually deploy a shutdown field before scanning your vessel. Without the proper countermeasures or escape preparation, carrying the wrong cargo can quickly become a death sentence.

In such a situation, abandoning your cargo may prevent the encounter from escalating further.

<summary>Titan Drive Component</summary>

### Titan Drive Components

Titan Drive Components can be obtained from Titan wrecks. They can be found randomly floating through space in the vicinity of a destroyed Titan.

Be aware that Thargoids may appear at every Titan wreck except Cocijo in Sol (Earth orbit). If you are unfamiliar with fighting them, lack the proper equipment, or simply do not feel confident engaging them, heading to Sol is highly recommended.

However, you will need a permit to enter the system. This permit is granted automatically once you reach the rank of Petty Officer with the Federation.

Even though Sol is free of Thargoids, it is not completely safe. Pirates will likely appear and scan your ship. They are generally not interested in Titan Drive Components or limpets, but they may attack if you are carrying other commodities in your cargo hold (for example Meta Alloys).

Because of this, I recommend using a ship with at least basic defenses, some weaponry, and enough speed or maneuverability to evade attackers if necessary.

**Warning**
To transport these materials safely, you will need a **Corrosion Resistant Cargo Rack**.

It is possible to carry them without one, but your ship will suffer continuous caustic damage that may eventually lead to its destruction.

<summary>Thargoid Sensor Fragments</summary>

### Thargoid Sensor Fragments

Thargoid Sensor Fragments can be found on planetary surfaces throughout the galaxy at locations known as Non-Human Signature Sites. These sites usually contain a crashed Thargoid Sensor with fragments scattered around the area.

The fragments can be collected using an SRV and, unlike intact Thargoid Sensors, they do **not** cause caustic damage to either your SRV or your ship. This is because the fragments are classified as **materials**, not commodities.

They are stored in your **Material Storage**, not in your ship's cargo hold.

Another way to obtain Sensor Fragments is to intentionally destroy an intact Thargoid Sensor and collect the fragments afterwards.

<summary>Weapon & Module Blueprint Fragments</summary>

### Weapon & Module Blueprint Fragments

To obtain a Weapon or Module Blueprint Fragment, you will need a **Guardian Relic**, which can usually be found somewhere within the Guardian Structure site itself.

Inside the site, locate the central altar structure. Once you approach it, the first two of six energy pylons will emerge from the ground.

Activate the pylons by shooting their energy cores with your SRV weapon. After activating the first two pylons, search the surrounding area for the remaining four pylons and activate them as well.

**Note:**  
As soon as the first pylon is activated, a timer will begin. Activating additional pylons will extend this timer.
If you fail to activate all six pylons before the timer expires, the process will reset and must be started again.

Once all six pylons are charged, return to the altar and jettison the Guardian Relic in front of it.

This will activate the structure and reveal a floating orb. Scan the orb using your ship's or SRV's Data Link Scanner to receive the Blueprint Fragment.

</details>


<details>
<summary>Vessel Blueprint Fragments</summary>

### Vessel Blueprint Fragments

To obtain a Vessel Blueprint Fragment, you first need to visit a system containing a **Guardian Beacon**. Guardian Beacons are large rotating structures located in space.

At the Beacon, activate it by shooting its three glowing nodes. Once activated, the Beacon will deploy an **Ancient Key**, which can then be collected using a Collector Limpet or manually through cargo scoop collection.

Take this Key to a specific Guardian Vessel Structure, usually located within the same system.

Just like at Weapon and Module Structure sites, activate all six energy pylons. However, instead of using a Guardian Relic, jettison the Ancient Key in front of the altar.

This will once again reveal a floating orb, which grants the Vessel Blueprint Fragment after being scanned.

---

**Warning**

Guardian Structure sites are protected by **Guardian Sentinels**, hostile drones that usually must be fought using your SRV.

</details>


<details>
<summary>Guardian Components</summary>

### Guardian Components

Guardian Components can be found within **Guardian Structures**. Some are hidden behind destructible panels, while others can be obtained from destroyed Guardian Sentinels.

Panels can be destroyed using your SRV weapon, revealing the component behind them.

Guardian Sentinels can also be destroyed using your SRV. However, be aware that they will fight back.

Examples of Guardian Components include:

- _Guardian Power Cell_
- _Guardian Power Conduit_
- _Guardian Sentinel Weapon Parts_
- _Guardian Technology Components_
- _Guardian Wreckage Components_

All of these can be collected using an SRV and are stored in your **Manufactured Material Storage**.

They do not require cargo space.

</details>

<summary>Guardian Scan Data</summary>

### Guardian Scan Data

Guardian Scan Data can be obtained by scanning **Guardian Obelisks**. These can be found at both **Guardian Structures** and **Guardian Ruins**. In general, Guardian Ruins tend to contain more obelisks than active Structure sites.

To successfully scan an obelisk and retrieve data, you will usually need a specific Guardian Artifact — or sometimes even a combination of artifacts — stored in your SRV cargo hold.

When approaching an obelisk, the required artifacts will be displayed automatically.

A complete guide covering all obelisk combinations would go beyond the scope of this material guide, so links to existing detailed guides will be provided instead.

Guardian Scan Data is divided into two categories:

- _Pattern Data_
- _Guardian Data_

Note

**Guardian Pattern Data** is primarily used for unlocking Guardian technology and modules.

**Guardian Data** is mainly used for the missions of the Engineer Ram Tah and for Guardian lore progression.
